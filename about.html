About Me

One of my favorite passions is to provide a product or service for others to enjoy. Below is a timeline of some of my most significant experiences that have matured me into making bigger and better projects for everyone!

Prior to High School

Although I wasnâ€™t actively creating projects, I would browse through Scratch a lot on my school-issued chromebook whenever I had the chance and remix community projects for fun. I loved playing indie games and had a knack for electronics as well.

September-December 2020

I decided to take an Exploring Computer Science (ECS) class that my school offered me. In this class, I would learn about how websites were built as well as a little bit of HTML and CSS.

January 2021

I would apply these skills and go on to create my first big project CritiVerse, a review website with the goal of providing users second opinions on products ranging from cars to laptops to baby essentials and more.

February-April 2021

The ECS class curriculum would teach me about scratch (drag and drop) level javascript. One of my favorite projects would be a short animation I created called The Scratch Men Awaken, where a group of German soldiers would discover living sculptures.

May 2021

Now that I knew the disciplines of programming, I started becoming more and more interested in another childhood game, Roblox. Roblox also allows the community to create games, and I thought it was amazing how these 3D games worked and behaved. I started trying Roblox Studio for the first time.

June 2021

For a while, I felt really overwhelmed with Roblox scripting: all I did was take free models made by the community. However, as I got more used to the environment, I started making an effort to watch tutorials about LUA from Youtubers like Alvinblox and TheDevKing. These individuals acted as mentors for me.

July 2021

As I sought to learn more about Roblox LUA, such as client and server events, player scripts, workspace scripts, and more, I started forming my first major creation: Titan Legends. The main goal of the game is to grow and defeat other players.

I also took notes during this time on properties such as data saving in a separate game called the Museum of LUA.

August 2021

To put my skills to the test, I decided to start a new game called Swamp Defense. The goal is to survive against a hoard of endless and angry crocodiles trying to attack your home: you are provided with a shotgun you can upgrade over time.

September-December 2021

With the new school year in session, I had to put down Roblox for a while. However, this did not stop me from programming, and I began to pick up Python. Learning from Youtubers such as CodingWithMosh and TechWithTim allowed me to utilize this language in Discord Bot development.

Additionally, I got to expand my knowledge with server uptime and data storage, something I was never concerned with before. TechWithTim specifically, helped me learn how to use mongoDB and Heroku to give my Discord bot more ability to help and entertain users.

January-April 2022

One of my high school teachers, Mr. Pham, recommended me to try out cybersecurity through a nation-wide competition called CyberStart. It was through this that I was able to learn about the other side of Computer Science: brute forcing, powerful operating systems like Linux, SQL injection, and so much more!

At the same time, I also began working with peers to design and prototype an invention that solves a global problem for UC San Diego's 2022 JTIC event. Targeting primarily landlocked people in Africa, we brainstormed and created the "Camel Cart" to make stroing, moving, and filtering water more efficent.

June-August 2022

I decided to reflect on the skills I have learned so far: one of the reasons why I started this website! Doing so has allowed me to expand my knowledge on features such as website animations and Javascript observers, and well as how to use github environments, pull requests, etc.

This portfolio was started during the summer of 2022: thanks for visiting!
